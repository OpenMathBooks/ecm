\(\frac{1}{x^2+x-1}=\frac{c}{x-r_1}+\frac{d}{x-r_2}\) gives us \(cx-cr_2+dx-dr_1=1\text{.}\) Thus \(c+d=0\text{,}\) and \(cr_2+dr_1 =-1\text{.}\) This gives us \(d=-c\) and so \(cr_2-cr_1=-1\text{,}\) which yields \(c=\frac{1}{r_1-r_2}\text{,}\) and \(d=\frac{1}{r_2-r_1}\text{.}\) By substitution, \(c=1/\sqrt{5}\) and \(d=-1/\sqrt{5}\text{.}\) This gives us

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{x^2+x-1} = \frac{1/\sqrt{5}}{x-\frac{-1+\sqrt{5}}{2}} + \frac{-1/\sqrt{5}}{x- \frac{-1-\sqrt{5}}{2}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
