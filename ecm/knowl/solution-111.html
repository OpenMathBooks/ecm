Solution 161.1.

The picture enumerator for choosing \(1\) or not choosing 1 is \(x_1+1\text{.}\) The picture enumerator for choosing or not choosing \(i\) is \(x_i+1\text{.}\) The picture enumerator for choosing all possible subsets of \([n]\) is \((x_1+1)(x_2+1)\cdots(x_n+1).\) We should substitute \(x\) for \(x_i\text{,}\) thus getting \((1+x)^n\text{.}\) Since the number of ways to choose an \(n\)-element subset is \(\binom{n}{k}\text{,}\) we have just proved the version of the binomial theorem that says

\begin{equation*} (x+1)^n=\sum_{i=0}^n \binom{n}{i}x^i. \end{equation*}
