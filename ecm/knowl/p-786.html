Frequently when we apply the principle of inclusion and exclusion, we will have a situation like that of part (d) of TaskÂ 138.d. That is, we will have a set \(A\) and subsets \(A_1, A_2, \ldots, A_n\) and we will want the size or the probability of the set of elements in \(A\) that are not in the union. This set is known as the complement of the union of the \(A_i\)s in \(A\text{,}\) and is denoted by \(A \setminus \bigcup_{i=1}^n A_i\text{,}\) or if \(A\) is clear from context, by \(\overline{\bigcup_{i=1}^n A_i}\text{.}\)
