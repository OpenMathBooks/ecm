Activity 19.
(a)

Explain why Proposition 1.2.5 is true.

(b)

An incomplete deck of cards only has four face cards (the Jack, King and Queen of diamonds and the King of hearts) and five black cards (the 2 through 6 of spades). A magician asks you to pick a card. How many choices do you have?

(c)

How exactly does the counting question above relate to Proposition 1.2.5? How exactly does it relate to the Sum Principle? Illustrate both by listing specific outcomes.

(d)

Make explicit the connection between the Sum Principle and Proposition 1.2.5. That is, justify the Sum Principle in terms of cardinality of sets.
