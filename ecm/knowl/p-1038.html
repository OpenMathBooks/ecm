By the extended binomial theorem,

\begin{equation*} \sqrt{1-4x}=(1-4x)^{1/2} = \sum_{i=0}^\infty \binom{1/2}{i}(-4x)^i= \sum_{i=0}^\infty \frac{(1/2)^{\underline{i}}}{i!}(-1)^i4^ix^i\text{.} \end{equation*}

The first term of this power series is 1, so to get a power series for \(y\text{,}\) we must take the negative square root so that the \(x\) in the denominator will cancel out. Thus \(y=-\frac{1}{2}\sum_{i=1}^\infty \frac{(1/2)^{\underline{i}}}{i!}(-1)^i4^ix^i\text{.}\) But \((1/2)^{\underline{i}}=(\frac{1}{2})(\frac{-1}{2})(\frac{-3}{2})(\frac{-5}{2})\cdots (\frac{-2i+3}{2})\text{,}\) so

\begin{align*} y \amp= -\frac{1}{2}\sum_{i=1}^\infty\frac{1\cdot3\cdot5\cdots(2i-3)}{i!}(-1)^{2i-1}2^ix^{i-1}\\ \amp= \sum_{i=1}^\infty \frac{(2i-2)!}{(i-1)!2^{i}i!}2^ix^{i-1}\\ \amp= \sum_{i=1}^\infty\frac{2i-2!}{i!(i-1)!}x^{i-1}\\ \amp= \sum_{j=0}^\infty\frac{2j!}{(j+1)!j!}x^j \end{align*}

giving us \(C_j=\frac{1}{j+1}\binom{2j}{j}\text{,}\) which is our earlier formula for the Catalan Number \(C_j\text{.}\)
