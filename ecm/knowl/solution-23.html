Solution 1.2.11.1.

In forming a derangement of \(1, 2, 3, \ldots, n\) the integer \(n\) can be placed in any of the \(n - 1\) spots \(1, 2, 3, \ldots, n - 1\text{,}\) say spot \(i\text{.}\) If \(i\) goes into spot \(n\) there are \(d_{n - 2}\) ways to finish it. If \(i\) does not go into spot \(n\) there are \(d_{n - 1}\) ways to complete the derangement.
