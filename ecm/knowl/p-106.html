First, an algebraic proof. We will see later that \(\binom{n}{k} = \frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!}\text{,}\) where \(r! = r \cdot (r-1) \cdot (r-2) \cdot\cdots\cdot 2\cdot 1\) (read “\(r\) factorial”). Using this algebraic formula, prove the identity \(\binom{n}{k} = \binom{n}{n-k}\text{.}\)
