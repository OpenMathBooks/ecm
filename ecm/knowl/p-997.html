\begin{align*} \frac{5x+1}{(x-3)(x+5)} \amp= \frac{c}{x-3} + \frac{d}{x+5}\\ \frac{5x+1}{(x-3)(x-5)} \amp= cx+5c+dx-3d \end{align*}

gives us

\begin{align*} 5x \amp= cx+dx\\ 1 \amp= 5c-3d\\ 5 \amp= c+d\\ 1 \amp= 5c-3d \end{align*}

This gives us \(16=8c\) so that \(c=2\) and then \(d=3\text{.}\)
