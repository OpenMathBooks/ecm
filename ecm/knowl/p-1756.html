Consider the graph \(G' = G - v\) (the graph resulting from removing \(v\) and all its incident edges from \(G\)). Since \(G'\) has \(n-1\) vertices, it is possible to properly 6-color its vertices. Looking back at \(G\text{,}\) we see that \(v\) is only adjacent to at most 5 vertices, so there are at most 5 colors we cannot use to color \(v\text{.}\) But we have 6 colors to work with, so we will be able to color \(v\text{,}\) and thus all of \(G\text{,}\) properly.
