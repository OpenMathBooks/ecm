Alternatively, you could first choose any one of the \(n\) people to serve as chair and then fill out the committee in \(\binom{n - 1}{k - 1}\) ways. There are \(n \binom{n - 1}{k - 1}\) ways to select a chaired subcommittee. Hence \(k \binom{n}{k} = n \binom{n - 1}{k - 1}\text{.}\)
