Solution 39.1.

The right-hand side, \(n!\text{,}\) gives the number of permutations of \(n\) objects. So the left-hand side must provide the same enumeration. The left side partitions the permutations according to how many elements are deranged (and the rest fixed). The term \(\binom{n}{i} d_{i} = \binom{n}{n - i} d_{i}\) gives the number of permutations of \(n\) where \(n - i\) elements are fixed and the remaining \(i\) elements are deranged. Summing over all \(i\) yields

\begin{equation*} \binom{n}{n} d_{0} + \binom{n}{n - 1} d_{1} + \cdots + \binom{n}{0} d_{n} = \binom{n}{0} d_{0} + \binom{n}{1} d_{1} + \cdots + \binom{n}{n} d_{n} = n! \end{equation*}
