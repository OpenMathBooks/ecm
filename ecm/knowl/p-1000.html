To have

\begin{equation*} \frac{ax+b}{(x-r_1)(x-r_2)} = \frac{c}{x-r_1} + \frac{d}{x-r_2} \end{equation*}

we must have

\begin{equation*} cx-r_2c+dx-r_2d = ax+b. \end{equation*}

This gives us the equations \(cx+dx=ax\) and \(-r_2c-r_1d=b\text{.}\) Since \(x\) can be any value, in particular it can be nonzero, so we can divide by it. This gives us the equations \(c+d=a\) and \(r_2c+r_1d=-b\text{.}\)
