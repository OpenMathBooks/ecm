Solution 302.c.1.

So, let's assume for a contradiction that your friend really has constructed a convex polyhedron. Then, we would know that the polyhedron has \(15\) faces, \((2\times 3+2\times 4+6\times 5+5\times 8)/2 = 42\) edges, and \(V=2+42-15=29\) vertices. Now, using the hint, we also know that \(3V\leq F\) but \(3\times 29\) is not in fact less than \(15\text{.}\) So we have a contradiction, and your friend is lying.
