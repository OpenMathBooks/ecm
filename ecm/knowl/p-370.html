Choose people in ordered pairs. The first person in an ordered pair serves first. There are \(2n(2n-1)\) ways to choose one pair, \((2n-2)(2n-3)\) ways to choose a second pair, and once \(k\) pairs have been chosen, there are \((2n-2k)(2n-2k-1)\) ways to choose the next pair. The number of lists of pairs we get in this way is \(\prod_{i=0}^{n-1} (2n-2i)(2n-2i-1) = (2n)!\text{.}\) However, each way of pairing people gets listed \(n!\) times since we see all possible length \(n\) lists of pairs. Therefore the number of actual pairings is \(\frac{(2n)!}{n!} = (2n)^{\underline{n}}\text{.}\)
