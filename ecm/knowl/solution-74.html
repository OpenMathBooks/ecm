Solution 118.a.1.

If a lattice path between \((0,0)\) and \((n,n)\) goes outside the triangle, it can only do so on an up-step. (A step from \((i,j)\) to \((i,j+1)\text{.}\)) And an up-step must originate at a point with integer coordinates. If \(j\lt i\) an up-step from \((i,j))\) cannot leave the triangle. Thus to leave the triangle, the up-step must leave from a point of the form \((i,i)\text{,}\) and go to \((i,i+1)\text{,}\) which is on the line \(y=x+1\text{.}\)
