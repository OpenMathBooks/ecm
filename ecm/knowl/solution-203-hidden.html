\(\displaystyle\prod_{i=1}^\infty 1+x^i=\prod_{i=1}^n \frac{1-x^{2i}}{1-x^i}=\frac{\prod_{i=1}^\infty1-x^{2i}}{\prod_{j=1}^\infty 1-x^j} =\prod_{i=j}^\infty \frac{1}{1-x^{2j-1}}\text{,}\) because all the terms in the numerator cancel with alternate terms in the denominator leaving only terms with odd powers of \(x\text{.}\) But

\begin{align*} \amp \prod_{j=1}^\infty\frac{1}{1-x^{2j-1}} = \prod_{j=1}^\infty \sum_{i=0}^\infty (x^{2j-1})^i\\ =\amp (1+x+x^{2\cdot1}+\cdots)(1+x^3+x^{2\cdot3}+\cdots)(1 +x^5+x^{2\cdot5}+\cdots)\cdots, \end{align*}

which is the generating function for partitions of integers into parts that are odd numbers.
