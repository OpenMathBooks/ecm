If we delete the block containing \(k\text{,}\) we get a partition of a subset of \([k-1]\text{.}\) Thus \(B_k\) is the sum over all subsets of \([k-1]\) of the number of partitions of that subset. This gives us \(B_k= \sum_{n=0}^{k-1}\binom{k-1}{n}B_n\text{.}\)

Alternatively, we can show by the same sort of argument that \(S(k,n)=\sum_{i=0}^{k-1} \binom{k-1}{i}S(i,n-1)\) and then use the fact that \(B_k =\sum_{n=0}^k S(k,n)\) to get the recurrence for \(B_k\text{.}\)
