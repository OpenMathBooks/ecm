\(L(k,n)\) is the number of broken permutations of a \(k\)-element set into \(n\) parts. Either \(k\) is in an ordered block with other numbers, or it is in a block by itself. If it is with other elements, it can go after any of the \(k-1\) other elements, or it can go at the beginning of any of the \(n\) blocks. If it is by itself, deleting it gives a broken permutation of a \(k-1\)-element set into \(n-1\) blocks. Thus \(L(k,n)=L(k-1,n-1) + (n+k-1)L(k-1,n)\text{.}\)
